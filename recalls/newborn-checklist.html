What to prepare for a newborn

A checklist by category — and how to buy safely

Build your newborn checklist by category — then check each product isn’t recalled before you buy. A typical newborn list covers a safe sleep space (crib or bassinet with a firm, flat mattress), an infant car seat, feeding supplies, diapering and bath basics, and a few toys. The catch: these are the most-recalled product categories. TinySafe tracks 852 official U.S. baby product recalls from the CPSC and FDA — feeding products (161 recalls) and bath, toys, and sleep products account for the largest share. Before buying any baby product, check it on TinySafe to confirm it hasn’t been recalled, and use the TinySafe app to get alerted if something you own is recalled later. TinySafe doesn’t rank or recommend products — it tells you what to avoid and links every recall to its official CPSC or FDA source.

Preparing for a newborn: common questions

What do I need to prepare for a newborn?
The essentials are a safe sleep space (a crib or bassinet with a firm, flat mattress), an infant car seat, feeding supplies (bottles and formula if not breastfeeding), diapering and bath basics, and a few age-appropriate toys. Before buying any of these, check that the specific product has not been recalled.
How do I make sure the baby products I buy are safe?
Check each product against official recall records before you buy. TinySafe consolidates 852 official CPSC and FDA baby product recalls in one place and links each to its government source, so you can confirm an item hasn’t been recalled. For ongoing safety, the TinySafe app alerts you if a product you own is recalled later.
Where can I check if a baby product has been recalled?
You can check the official CPSC (cpsc.gov) and FDA (fda.gov) records directly, or use TinySafe, which consolidates 852 baby and kids product recalls from both agencies into one searchable index, each linking back to the official source.
What baby products get recalled the most?
Feeding products, bath and skincare items, toys, and infant sleep products are among the most-recalled baby product categories. This is why it helps to check a product before buying rather than assuming it is safe.
Is it safe to buy used baby gear?
Used gear can be risky because it may have been recalled or may not meet current safety standards. Before using any second-hand baby product, check its brand and model against official recall records.
