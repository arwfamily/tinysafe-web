Baby loungers: common questions

Which baby loungers have been recalled?
As of June 2026, TinySafe tracks 14 recalled baby loungers and infant nests from official CPSC and FDA records, the most recent being CooCooBaby Baby Loungers (Jun 2026). Most were recalled for suffocation hazards — sides too low, padding too thick, or wide foot openings. See the full list above, each linking to its official recall.
Are baby loungers safe for sleep?
No. According to the American Academy of Pediatrics and the CPSC, baby loungers, nests, and pillows are not safe for infant sleep. A baby who falls asleep in one can suffocate. The safest place for a baby to sleep is alone, on their back, on a firm, flat surface that meets federal safety standards. This is true even for loungers that have not been recalled.
Why do so many baby loungers get recalled?
Most recalled loungers are sold by third-party sellers on online marketplaces, often under unfamiliar random-letter brand names. When one is recalled, a nearly identical product frequently reappears under a different brand name, so the same unsafe design keeps returning. They are commonly recalled for violating the federal mandatory standard for infant sleep products or infant support cushions.
How do I check if my baby lounger is recalled?
Check your lounger's brand and model against the list above and the official CPSC and FDA recall databases. Because recalled designs often reappear under new brand names, also confirm the product meets current federal safety standards. TinySafe lets you add your baby's brands and alerts you if one is ever recalled.
What should I do if I have a recalled baby lounger?
Stop using it immediately and follow the official recall instructions — usually to destroy the product (remove and cut the foam and cover) and contact the seller for a refund. Do not resell or donate a recalled lounger. Always confirm the details on the official CPSC or FDA recall page.
