Button battery recalls: toys & products that can be swallowed

A swallowed button battery is a medical emergency — see what's been recalled

A swallowed button or coin battery can cause severe internal burns within hours — it is a medical emergency. TinySafe tracks 43 baby and kids products recalled for button-battery hazards, mostly light-up toys, novelties, and games with batteries that aren't secured. Under Reese's Law, products with these batteries must have child-resistant compartments. Check the products below, keep loose batteries out of reach, and seek emergency care immediately if you suspect a child swallowed one.

What official guidance says
From the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC).
Reese's Law
Under Reese’s Law, consumer products containing button or coin batteries must have child-resistant battery compartments and warning labels. Some products have been recalled for non-compliant compartments.
Why button batteries are so dangerous
If swallowed, a button battery can react with saliva and cause severe internal burns within two hours. Symptoms can be vague, so any suspected swallowing is treated as an emergency.
If you suspect a child swallowed a battery
Seek emergency care immediately or call your local emergency number or poison control. TinySafe tracks recalls and does not provide medical advice.
