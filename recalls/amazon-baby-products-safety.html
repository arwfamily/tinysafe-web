Are baby products on Amazon safe?

Recalled baby & kids products sold on Amazon — and what to check

Many baby products on Amazon are fine — but a notable number sold by third-party sellers have been recalled for failing U.S. safety rules. TinySafe tracks 108 baby and kids products sold on Amazon that were recalled, and the large majority were recalled for violating a mandatory U.S. federal safety standard — for example, banned small parts, non-compliant infant sleep products, or unsafe button-battery compartments. Most are toys and baby gear. Before buying a baby product on Amazon, check it against the recalls below and confirm with the CPSC. A product not listed here is not confirmed safe.

What to check before buying baby products on Amazon
Based on U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) standards.
Watch third-party and overseas sellers
Many recalled items come from third-party sellers, often overseas, whose products may not meet U.S. federal safety standards even when sold on a major marketplace.
Small parts and magnets
Toys for children under 3 must not contain small parts that can be swallowed, and high-powered magnets are restricted. A large share of recalled Amazon toys failed these mandatory standards.
Infant sleep and button batteries
Infant loungers and sleep products must meet federal sleep-safety rules, and products with button batteries must be child-resistant under Reese’s Law. Check these against official recalls.
