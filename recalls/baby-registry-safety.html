Baby registry safety: check recalls before you add it

We don’t recommend products — we show you what’s been recalled, by category

We won't tell you what to buy — we'll show you what's been recalled, so 'has this been recalled?' is one honest thing you can check before you add it to your registry. Most 'best baby products' lists are recommendations or ads. TinySafe doesn't rank or recommend products — we have no review data. What we do have is every baby and kids product recalled by the CPSC and FDA (852 and counting), organized below by the registry categories parents actually shop. A product not listed isn't confirmed safe — absence just means no recall on record. For anything that isn't recalled, ask your pediatrician.

Common questions

Can you tell me what to buy for my baby — the best products?
No — and any source that confidently ranks 'the best' baby products is usually guessing or selling something. TinySafe has no review data and doesn't recommend or rank products. What we can do is show you, objectively, which products in each registry category have been recalled and why, so 'has this been recalled?' is one honest factor you can check before you add something.
Which baby registry categories have the most recalls?
Feeding (bottles and formula), toys and teethers, bath and skincare, and safe-sleep products account for the most recalls in our data. Sleep products are the highest-risk category because some recalls follow infant deaths. Each category above links to the specific recalled products.
Are unbranded or 'alphabet-soup' Amazon baby products safe to add?
Products from unfamiliar random-letter brands and third-party sellers don't always meet U.S. safety standards, and many have been recalled — a common worry parents raise about gifted or low-cost registry items. If you can't identify a real brand or find safety information, check it against official recalls before using it, especially for anything that goes in a baby's mouth or that they sleep in. See our Amazon baby products page.
Is it safe to buy or accept secondhand baby gear?
Often yes, but check the exact model first — recalled cribs, bassinets, loungers, swings, and car seats are frequently resold by people who don't know they were recalled. Never use a recalled sleep product. Look up the model on this site or at cpsc.gov before using a hand-me-down.
How do I actually check a specific product before adding it?
Find it on this page by category, or use the TinySafe recall index. Each entry links to the official FDA or CPSC record so you can confirm the affected models or lots. For a step-by-step method, see our guide on how to check for baby recalls.
Do I have to check every product manually forever?
No — that's the point of the app. Checking a page only shows past recalls. TinySafe lets you add the products you actually registered for or own, and alerts you the moment one is recalled, across CPSC and FDA. It's launching soon and you can join the waitlist.
