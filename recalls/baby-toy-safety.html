Toy recalls: choking, magnets & button batteries

The recalled toys parents actually search for — by hazard

The toy hazards that send kids to the ER are swallowed high-powered magnets and button batteries — plus choking on small parts. Before you buy a toy or add one to a registry, it's worth a quick recall check. TinySafe tracks 97 recalled baby and kids toys: 30 for magnet ingestion, 21 for button batteries, and 34 for choking. What official guidance says
From the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC).
Small parts and choking (under 3)
Toys for children under 3 must not contain small parts that can be swallowed. A rough test: if a part fits inside a toilet-paper tube, it can be a choking hazard for a young child.
High-powered magnets are a medical emergency
If a child swallows two or more high-powered magnets, they can attract through the intestinal walls and cause serious injury. The CPSC restricts these magnets; seek emergency care if you suspect a child swallowed any. TinySafe tracks the recalls, not medical care.
Button batteries (Reese's Law)
Swallowed button or coin batteries can cause severe internal burns within hours. Under Reese’s Law, toys with these batteries must have child-resistant compartments. Keep batteries out of reach and seek emergency care immediately if you suspect a child swallowed one.

Common questions

Which toys are most dangerous for babies and toddlers?
The most serious toy hazards are swallowed high-powered magnets and button batteries, which are medical emergencies, and choking on small parts for children under 3. TinySafe tracks toys recalled for each of these, grouped by hazard on this page.
Are baby toys from Amazon, Temu, or unknown sellers safe?
Toys from third-party and overseas sellers don't always meet U.S. safety standards, and many have been recalled for choking, magnet, or lead hazards. Before buying or keeping a gifted toy from an unfamiliar brand, check it against official recalls. TinySafe has dedicated pages for recalled baby products sold on Amazon and on Temu/Shein.
Why are magnet toys (magnetic balls, tiles, chess) dangerous?
If a child swallows more than one high-powered magnet, the magnets can attract through the intestines and cause serious internal injury. Many magnetic balls, sets, and games have been recalled for this; the recalled ones are listed on this page.
Are button batteries in toys dangerous?
Yes. A swallowed button or coin battery can cause severe internal burns within hours. Toys with these batteries are regulated under Reese’s Law, and some have been recalled for non-compliant compartments. If you suspect a child swallowed one, seek emergency care immediately.
What about lead or 'non-toxic' concerns in baby toys?
Some toys have been recalled for elevated lead in paint or materials. TinySafe tracks toys recalled for lead, and a dedicated lead-in-baby-products page covers this. We report official recalls only — we don't rate toys as 'clean' or 'non-toxic,' since that isn't something recall data can confirm.
How do I check a toy is safe before buying or gifting it?
Check the brand and product against official CPSC recalls before you buy or add it to a registry, and for children under 3, avoid toys with small parts, loose magnets, or accessible button batteries. Recalled toys are listed on this page with official sources.
My child may have swallowed a magnet or button battery — what do I do?
Treat it as a medical emergency and seek care immediately or call your local emergency number or poison control. TinySafe tracks recalls and does not provide medical advice.
