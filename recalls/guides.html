Common questions

Where can I check if a baby product has been recalled?
You can check the official CPSC (cpsc.gov) and FDA (fda.gov), or use TinySafe, which consolidates official U.S. baby and kids product recalls into one place and organizes them into guides by topic — safe sleep, formula, toys, lead, and more — each linked back to its government source.
How do I know which baby products are safe to buy?
Before buying, check the specific product against official recalls. TinySafe groups recalls by the questions parents ask — such as safe sleep, formula, and toy safety — so you can quickly see what's been recalled in a category. TinySafe reports official recalls; it does not rank or recommend products.
What baby products get recalled most often?
Infant sleep products, formula and baby food, toys, and items with lead are among the most-recalled categories. Each has its own guide on TinySafe with the recalled products and official sources.

Recalls get announced. TinySafe tells you.

The CPSC and FDA publish recalls — but no one tells you when a brand you use is recalled. TinySafe does. Add your baby’s brands and get an alert the moment one is recalled. Launching soon on iOS.
