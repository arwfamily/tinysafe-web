Are crib bumpers safe? Why they're banned and what's been recalled

Safety guide · Updated Jun 2026

SHORT ANSWER

No. Padded crib bumpers are banned from sale in the US under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act (2022) because they pose a suffocation, entrapment, and strangulation risk to infants. The safest crib has nothing in it but a fitted sheet. If you see a padded or "braided" bumper for sale, do not buy it — including secondhand.

Why no crib bumper is safe
Padded bumpers are banned, not just recalled

Under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act, padded crib bumpers can no longer be legally sold in the US. Infants lack the strength to turn their heads away from soft surfaces, so a bumper can block their airway. "Breathable," "mesh," and "braided" bumpers are still risky and not recommended — the AAP advises a completely bare crib: a firm, flat mattress with only a fitted sheet. If your crib has a bumper, remove it.

A warning about "best crib bumper" lists
Popular doesn't mean safe

Some online listings and "best crib bedding" sets still include padded or braided bumpers, despite the federal ban. A bumper being sold, stylish, or part of a matching nursery set does not make it legal or safe. Skip any bumper and keep the crib bare.

Why so many get recalled
Many recalled bumpers are sold by third-party online sellers who continue listing banned products, often as "braided" or decorative bedding. When one listing is removed, a similar one reappears under a new brand. That's why a bare crib — not a "safer" bumper — is the only safe choice.

Crib bumpers: common questions
Are crib bumpers safe?
No. Padded crib bumpers are banned from sale in the US under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act because they pose suffocation, entrapment, and strangulation risks. The AAP recommends a bare crib with only a fitted sheet.
Are crib bumpers illegal?
Padded crib bumpers can no longer be legally manufactured or sold in the US as of 2022. Some sellers still list them illegally online, so you may still see them for sale.
Are mesh or breathable crib bumpers safe?
Safety experts and the AAP still advise against all crib bumpers, including mesh or breathable types. The safest crib has nothing in it but a fitted sheet.
What can I use instead of a crib bumper?
Nothing is needed. A firm, flat mattress with a fitted sheet is the safest sleep surface. If you're worried about limbs slipping through slats, a properly sized crib that meets current standards already has safe slat spacing.
What should I do if I have a crib bumper?
Remove it from the crib and don't resell or donate it. Check the recall list above and the official CPSC database, and keep the crib bare.
