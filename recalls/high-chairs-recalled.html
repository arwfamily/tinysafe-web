What to check before you buy
We don't rate products "safe." But recalled high chairs failed on the same points — so here's what to check on any high chair, even one that hasn't been recalled.
A working 5-point harness with crotch restraint
7 of 13 recalls involved a missing or faulty restraint. A child can stand up and fall, or slide down, without one. Buckle it every time.
No gap between seat and tray
8 of 13 recalls involved an entrapment or fall risk from openings. A child shouldn't be able to slip down or get a limb trapped.
Legs and frame that lock securely
Some recalled chairs had legs that detached when screws loosened. Check that all hardware is tight and the frame is stable before each use.
A stable, wide base that won't tip
A narrow or wobbly base can tip when a child pushes against a table. Test stability before buying.
Never leave your child unattended in it
Even with a harness, a child can wriggle or climb. Stay within reach during meals.
A warning about "best high chair" lists
Popular doesn't mean safe

Some "best high chair" lists feature budget or convertible models later recalled for restraint or entrapment problems. A high chair being affordable, multi-function, or highly reviewed doesn't guarantee it meets the safety standard. Cross-check any pick against the recall list above.

Why so many get recalled
Many recalled high chairs are convertible or "X-in-1" models sold by third-party online sellers under unfamiliar brand names. When one is recalled for a missing restraint or entrapment gap, a near-identical product reappears under a new brand. The same design flaws keep returning.

High chairs: common questions
Are baby high chairs safe?
Most are, but recalls are common for missing restraints, detaching legs, and gaps a child can slip through. Choose one with a working 5-point harness and no seat-to-tray gap, and avoid the recalled models above.
Which high chairs have been recalled?
TinySafe tracks recalled high chairs from official CPSC records, most for fall and entrapment hazards. See the full list above, each linking to its official recall.
What should I look for in a high chair?
A working 5-point harness with a crotch restraint, no gap between seat and tray, securely locking legs and frame, and a stable wide base. Always buckle your child and stay nearby.
Why do high chairs get recalled?
The most common reasons are a missing or faulty restraint system, legs that detach, and openings between the seat and tray where a child can become entrapped, all posing fall or entrapment risks.
Is it safe to buy a used high chair?
Be cautious. Check the brand against the recall list above and the official CPSC database, confirm all hardware is present and tight, and make sure it has a working crotch restraint.
