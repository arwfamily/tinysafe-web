Safe sleep for babies

What to use, and what's been recalled

The safest place for a baby to sleep is alone, on their back, on a firm, flat surface — a crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets federal safety standards. According to the AAP and the CPSC, baby loungers, inclined sleepers, crib bumpers, pillows, and other soft or padded products are not safe for infant sleep. They are a leading category of recalls: TinySafe tracks 37 infant sleep-surface products recalled (2024–2026), and 84% were recalled for a suffocation or entrapment hazard. The recalled products to avoid are listed below, each with its official CPSC source. A product not listed here is not confirmed safe — always check current AAP safe-sleep guidance and official CPSC and FDA records.

What official guidance says
From the American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) and the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC).
A firm, flat surface only
The AAP and CPSC recommend placing a baby to sleep on a firm, flat, non-inclined surface in a crib, bassinet, or play yard. Soft surfaces, inclines, and padding increase suffocation risk.
Federal standard for infant sleep products
Under the CPSC’s federal safety standard for infant sleep products, products marketed for infant sleep must have a sleep surface angle of 10 degrees or less. Inclined sleepers and many loungers do not meet this standard.
Loungers are for supervised awake time, not sleep
Baby loungers and nursing pillows are intended for supervised, awake use only — never for sleep, and never inside a crib or bassinet.
Nothing else in the sleep space
Keep crib bumpers, pillows, blankets, and soft toys out of the sleep area. Several crib bumpers and padded products have been recalled for suffocation risk.

Safe baby sleep: common questions

Where should my baby sleep?
On a firm, flat surface in a crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets federal safety standards — alone, on their back, with no pillows, bumpers, or soft bedding. This is the guidance from the AAP and CPSC.
Are baby loungers safe for sleep?
No. Baby loungers are not safe for infant sleep and are intended for supervised awake time only. TinySafe tracks 13 recalled baby loungers, most for a suffocation hazard.
Which infant sleep products have been recalled?
TinySafe tracks 37 recalled infant sleep-surface products (2024–2026), including baby loungers, crib bumpers, crib and play-yard mattresses, bassinets, and inclined sleepers. 84% were recalled for a suffocation or entrapment hazard. The full grouped list with official CPSC sources is on this page.
Are crib bumpers safe?
Federal guidance advises against padded crib bumpers because of suffocation risk, and several have been recalled. TinySafe tracks 8 recalled crib bumpers.
How do I check if my baby's sleep product has been recalled?
Find the product’s brand and model in the list on this page, or search the TinySafe recall index. Each entry links to the official CPSC recall record so you can confirm the affected models.
