Baby formula recalls

Researching which formula to use? See which brands have been recalled — and why

If you're choosing an infant formula — or switching because one doesn't seem to agree with your baby — it helps to know which brands have been recalled and why. TinySafe tracks 53 infant formula recalls from the FDA, including major and sensitive or hypoallergenic formulas. The most common reasons are bacteria such as Cronobacter and Salmonella, and botulism risk, which can be especially dangerous for infants. Brands recalled include Similac (Abbott), Enfamil, ByHeart, Gerber, and gentle/sensitive lines. If your baby has symptoms like changes in stool or gas, talk to your pediatrician about whether to switch — and if you do switch, check the new formula hasn't been recalled. A formula not listed here is not confirmed safe; always check the latest FDA recalls.

What official guidance says
From the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA).
Cronobacter and Salmonella
The FDA warns that powdered infant formula can carry Cronobacter and Salmonella, which can cause serious illness in infants. Follow safe preparation and storage instructions, and discard recalled lots.
Switching formula? Check with your pediatrician first
Signs a formula may not agree with a baby — such as changes in stool, gas, or fussiness — can have many causes. Talk to your pediatrician before switching, especially for specialized or hypoallergenic formula. TinySafe does not give medical advice; it tracks recalls.
Check the lot number and date
Formula recalls usually apply to specific lot numbers and expiration dates printed on the container. Match yours against the official FDA record before using or discarding.

Common questions

My baby's formula doesn't seem to agree with them — what should I do?
Symptoms like changes in stool color, gas, or fussiness can have many causes, so talk to your pediatrician about whether and how to switch formula. TinySafe doesn't give medical advice — but if you do switch, you can check here that the new formula hasn't been recalled.
Which sensitive or hypoallergenic formulas have been recalled?
Several gentle, sensitive, and hypoallergenic formulas have been recalled, including products like Enfamil Nutramigen, Gerber Good Start SoothePro, and Similac Sensitive — often for bacteria. The full list grouped by type, with official FDA sources, is on this page.
Which baby formula brands have been recalled?
TinySafe tracks FDA infant formula recalls across major brands, including Similac (Abbott), Enfamil, ByHeart, Gerber Good Start, and store brands. The full list grouped by brand, with official FDA sources, is on this page.
Is ByHeart formula safe? Was Similac or Enfamil recalled?
ByHeart has been recalled more than once, including for bacteria and botulism risk. Abbott (Similac) and Mead Johnson (Enfamil) products also appear in FDA recalls. Check the specific product and lot number on this page against the official FDA source.
Are European formulas like HiPP and Holle FDA-regulated?
European formulas such as HiPP and Holle are not reviewed by the FDA the same way U.S. formulas are, and some have appeared in U.S. recalls or import alerts. Check specific products against official recalls, and talk to your pediatrician before using imported formula.
