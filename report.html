The real shift

Recalls aren't exploding. The danger moved online — and injuries are climbing.

Across all consumer products, the CPSC issued 305 recalls in 2024 — slightly fewer than the year before. The number of recalls held roughly flat. What didn't hold flat was the harm.

Source: U.S. PIRG Education Fund analysis of CPSC data, 2025. The takeaway for parents isn't that more products are being pulled — it's that the riskiest ones are increasingly the ones bought online, from sellers that are hard to keep track of.

Where the risk concentrates

Two aisles account for most of it.

Recalls aren't spread evenly across the nursery. The overwhelming majority land in toys & gear and in food & formula — the two things a baby touches and eats most.

The hazards that matter

The two most common are the two most deadly.

Excluding generic notices, the leading specific hazards are suffocation and choking — the exact mechanisms that make infants most vulnerable. Button batteries and high-powered magnets follow close behind.

The blind spot

Many ship from marketplace sellers you can't track.

A large share of recent recalls trace to third-party sellers on Amazon, AliExpress, Temu and similar marketplaces — names a parent never remembers buying from, with no reliable way to be notified when one is pulled.

Methodology. Figures cover 602 consumer-facing baby and toddler product recalls (toys & gear, food & formula, skincare, oral care, wipes, sunscreen) compiled from the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and the Food & Drug Administration. Clinical and hospital-grade medical-device recalls are excluded so the figures reflect what families actually buy. Hazard counts exclude unspecified/general notices. We make no year-over-year trend claim: CPSC's own total recall counts were essentially flat (323 in 2023, 305 in 2024), so apparent year-to-year swings in any single dataset can reflect data coverage rather than real-world change. The figures here describe the composition of recalls — what gets recalled, why, and how serious — which is robust to that.

Sources: CPSC Recalls · FDA Recalls & Safety Alerts. Last updated June 2026.
